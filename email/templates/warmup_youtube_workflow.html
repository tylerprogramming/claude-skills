I used to spend 2-3 hours on research, scripting, and thumbnails every time I made a YouTube video. And if I'm being honest, half the time I'd skip the research and just wing it because I didn't have the time.

So I built a system that does all of it from my terminal. Four commands, and I have a complete video package ready to film.

Here's what each one does:

/yt-search Pulls the top-performing videos for any topic in the last 30 days, sorted by views. I can instantly see what's working, what formats are trending, and where the gaps are.
/transcribe Takes any YouTube URL, downloads the audio, and transcribes it with Whisper. Then I can study exactly what hooks, structure, and pacing the best videos are using.
/yt The big one. It does web research, asks me a few questions about my angle, then generates titles, hooks, a full script, a YouTube description, and a filming guide. Everything I need to sit down and film.
/thumbnail Generates multiple thumbnail variants using AI image generation. I pick the best one and I'm done. No Photoshop, no Canva.

What used to take me an entire afternoon now takes about 20 minutes. And the output is actually better because I'm not cutting corners on research anymore.
